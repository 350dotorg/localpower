Host an energy meeting

Energy meetings bring together people who are already part of a community, like parents in a PTA, parishioners in a church, or people in a book club. The meetings bring the idea of saving energy to this community, and get members excited about taking action to reduce their use.

How do I host?

Repower at Home's toolkit makes it easy to invite guests, manage RSVPs, and hold a great event. Get started by downloading the host guide. You can also review other materials that will help you organize and run your event. Check out the materials page.

Create an Event

What happens at a meeting?

The meeting introduces guests to four actions almost anyone can do. Guests make commitments to take these actions, and brainstorm next steps they can take together to get even more people involved.

Who hosts meetings?

Anyone, from engineers to English teachers, can host a meeting. You don’t need to be an energy efficiency expert to host – in fact, you don’t need to have any background in the topic at all. The materials page will give you all the information you need to run an event and share your enthusiasm with your community.

What if I need help?

A field organizer will support you as you run your meeting. Use the contact links on the right of this page to get in touch with a field organizer.

Need some help?

If you would like to talk to a real person about hosting an energy meeting you can send us an email or we can give you a call.
